ENVIRONMENTAL DISASTER RESPONSE PROTOCOLS

WILDFIRE EMERGENCY RESPONSE

THREAT LEVELS
Level 1 - Watch: Fire conditions exist. Be aware and prepared.
Level 2 - Warning: Fire is near. Prepare to leave immediately.
Level 3 - Emergency (Evacuate NOW): Leave immediately. Your life is in danger.

EVACUATION PROCEDURES
1. Immediate actions when Level 3 declared:
   - Gather Go-Bag (documents, medications, water, phone charger)
   - Close all windows and doors (slows fire entry)
   - Turn off propane/gas at source
   - Leave lights on (helps firefighters see house through smoke)
   - Follow designated evacuation routes ONLY
   
2. Go-Bag essentials:
   - Water (1 gallon/person/day for 3 days)
   - Food (3-day supply, non-perishable)
   - Medications and first aid kit
   - N95 respirators (smoke protection)
   - Phone charger and backup battery
   - Important documents (ID, insurance, prescriptions)
   - Cash (ATMs may not work)
   - Change of clothes
   - Flashlight and batteries

3. Shelter-in-place if evacuation blocked:
   - Choose interior room with fewest windows
   - Seal door gaps with wet towels
   - Turn off all fans and HVAC
   - Use N95 mask indoors

WILDFIRE SMOKE HEALTH PROTOCOLS
When AQI > 150 (Unhealthy):
- Sensitive groups stay indoors with windows closed
- Run air purifier on HEPA setting
- Avoid strenuous outdoor activity

When AQI > 200 (Very Unhealthy):
- Everyone stays indoors
- N95 mask required for any outdoor activity
- Seek medical care if experiencing: difficulty breathing, chest pain, confusion

When AQI > 300 (Hazardous):
- Emergency conditions
- Consider evacuation if sustained over 6+ hours
- Contact emergency management for assistance

FLOOD EMERGENCY RESPONSE

FLOOD WATCH vs WARNING
- Watch: Flooding is possible. Stay informed and be ready to act.
- Warning: Flooding is occurring or imminent. Take action now.
- Emergency: Extreme flooding. Immediate threat to life.

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS - FLOOD WARNING
1. Move to higher ground immediately
2. Never walk, swim, or drive through flood waters
   - 6 inches of moving water can knock down an adult
   - 1 foot of water can float most vehicles
   - 2 feet of rushing water can carry away most vehicles
3. Disconnect electrical appliances (if safe to do so)
4. Avoid contact with floodwater (contaminated with sewage, chemicals)
5. If trapped in building: go to roof, signal for help

FLASH FLOOD SPECIFIC ACTIONS
- Flash floods can occur within minutes to hours of heavy rainfall
- Warning signs: Rapid rise in water levels, roaring sounds upstream, debris-laden water
- Immediate evacuation when warning issued - do NOT wait to see water
- Never camp in dry riverbeds (flash floods can arrive with no local rain)

POST-FLOOD SAFETY
- Do not return home until authorities declare it safe
- Document damage before cleaning (insurance)
- Wear rubber boots and gloves when cleaning
- Discard all food that contacted floodwater
- Check structure for damage before entering
- Be aware of mold growth within 24-48 hours

EXTREME HEAT EMERGENCY RESPONSE

HEAT WATCH/WARNING CRITERIA
Heat Advisory: Heat index 100-104°F for ≥2 days
Excessive Heat Watch: Conditions favorable for Heat Warning
Excessive Heat Warning: Heat index ≥105°F for ≥2 hours AND overnight temperatures ≥75°F

HEAT ILLNESS PROGRESSION
1. Heat Cramps: Muscle spasms
   - Move to cool place, drink water with electrolytes, stretch muscles

2. Heat Exhaustion: 
   - Symptoms: Heavy sweating, weakness, cool/pale/clammy skin, weak pulse, nausea
   - Action: Move to cool location, loosen clothing, apply cool wet cloths, sip water
   - Seek medical care if no improvement in 30 minutes

3. Heat Stroke (MEDICAL EMERGENCY):
   - Symptoms: Hot/red/dry or damp skin, rapid strong pulse, confusion, body temp >103°F
   - Action: Call 911 immediately, cool rapidly by any means available
   - This is life-threatening - do NOT delay calling emergency services

HEAT VULNERABILITY
Highest risk groups:
- Adults 65 and older (reduced sweating ability, medications)
- Infants and young children (high surface-to-mass ratio, can't communicate distress)
- People without air conditioning
- Outdoor workers (farmers, construction, landscaping)
- Athletes training outdoors
- People with chronic conditions (heart disease, obesity, mental illness)
- People taking diuretics, antihistamines, beta-blockers

COMMUNITY RESPONSE
- Open cooling centers: Libraries, community centers, malls
- Check on elderly neighbors daily
- Never leave children or pets in vehicles (car temps can reach 120°F+ quickly)
- Emergency services should prioritize vulnerability checks

DROUGHT AND WATER SCARCITY RESPONSE

DROUGHT STAGES AND RESPONSE
D0 - Abnormally Dry: Voluntary conservation requested
D1 - Moderate Drought: Restrictions on outdoor watering
D2 - Severe Drought: Significant restrictions, emergency planning
D3 - Extreme Drought: Critical shortages, water rationing
D4 - Exceptional Drought: Emergency declarations, supply deliveries

WATER CONSERVATION MEASURES BY STAGE
Stage 1: Reduce usage 10-15%
- Fix leaks immediately
- Water lawns early morning or evening only (2 days/week)
- Use dishwasher/laundry only when full

Stage 2: Reduce usage 25-30%
- No outdoor watering except food gardens
- No car washing
- Limit showers to 5 minutes

Stage 3: Reduce usage 40%
- No outdoor water use permitted
- Restaurants serve water only on request
- Pool filling prohibited

Stage 4: Emergency rationing
- Government-controlled distribution
- Priority: Drinking, cooking, sanitation only
- Agricultural use severely restricted

EARTHQUAKE AND GEOHAZARD RESPONSE

DURING EARTHQUAKE
1. DROP: Get on hands and knees before shaking knocks you down
2. COVER: Get under sturdy desk/table, or against interior wall, away from windows
3. HOLD ON: Stay until shaking stops

AFTER EARTHQUAKE - ENVIRONMENTAL HAZARDS
- Gas leaks: Smell gas → open windows, leave building, don't use electrical switches
- Chemical spills: Infrastructure damage can cause industrial accidents
- Landslides: Common in mountainous areas after earthquakes
- Tsunamis: If earthquake felt in coastal area, move inland immediately
- Liquefaction: Sandy soils can behave like liquid, damaging structures

CHEMICAL AND INDUSTRIAL EMERGENCY RESPONSE

HAZMAT INCIDENT PROTOCOLS
1. Identify: What chemical? (MSDS sheets, placards, emergency contacts)
2. Notify: Call 911, report location, chemical name if known
3. Isolate: Establish safety perimeter (typically 300m minimum)
4. Protect: Upwind positioning, shelter-in-place if advised

SHELTER-IN-PLACE FOR CHEMICAL EMERGENCY
- Go inside a building immediately
- Close all windows, doors, fireplace dampers
- Turn off fans, air conditioning, heating
- Move to interior room above ground level
- Listen to emergency radio for all-clear
- If airborne chemical exposure: cover nose/mouth with wet cloth

RADIATION EMERGENCY (Nuclear/Radiological)
- Get inside: Brick/concrete buildings provide 10x better protection than wood
- Stay inside: Radiation decreases dramatically with distance from source
- Stay tuned: Follow official instructions
- Potassium Iodide (KI) only if instructed by authorities (protects thyroid)

ENVIRONMENTAL MONITORING THRESHOLDS FOR ALERTING

Air Quality Index:
- >100: Alert sensitive populations
- >150: Issue public health advisory
- >200: Declare air quality emergency, open air refuges
- >300: Hazardous emergency, consider evacuation of vulnerable

Heat Index:
- >90°F: Issue heat advisory for sensitive groups
- >100°F: Open cooling centers
- >105°F: Excessive heat warning, check on vulnerable
- >110°F: Emergency heat crisis, mandatory cooling center referrals

Wind Speed (Wildfire Risk):
- >15 mph: Elevated fire spread risk
- >25 mph: High fire danger, red flag conditions
- >45 mph: Extreme fire danger, pre-position resources

Flood Stage:
- Action Stage: Rivers at bankfull stage, minor flooding
- Flood Stage: Flooding of low areas
- Moderate Flood Stage: Flooding of structures/roads
- Major Flood Stage: Widespread inundation, evacuations
